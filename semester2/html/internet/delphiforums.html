Delphi

The forerunner of forums

The company “Kussmaul Encyclopedia” was launched in October 1981 by Wes Kussmaul and it was the first internet service to feature an ASCII-based1 encyclopedia, E-mail exchange, and a primitive chat. This company later grew to become what it is now, “Delphi”. The Kussmaul Encyclopedia was as well the world's first commercially available computerized encyclopedia and it was actually a complete home computer system, for which you had to choose the TRS-80 Color Computer2 or the Apple II, with a 300-bps modem that dialed up to a VAX computer hosting the online encyclopedia database.

The first encyclopedia

A 1989 Delphi menu.

People soon noticed the revolution it meant: searching information and “wandering around knowledge” was easy as never before. The encyclopedia’s cross-referencing system turned every occurrence of a word that was the name of an entry in the encyclopedia itself into an hypertext link, and all of this in 1981. On March 15, 1983, the Delphi name was first used by General Videotex Corporation. Its forums were text-based, and you could access to them via Telenet, Sprintnet, Tymnet, Uninet, and Datapac. Delphi was then extended to Argentina in 1985, through a partnership with the Argentine IT company Siscotel S.A, and finally, Delphi partnered with ASCII Corp. of Japan to open online services in 1991.
