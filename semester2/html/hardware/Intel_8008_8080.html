Intel 8008 and Intel 8080

Intel 8008 chip

Intel 8008

The Intel 8008 is the world's first 8 bit microprocessor, which would perform arithmetic and logic operations on 8 bit words. It was introduced in April 19723. It was an 8-bit CPU with an external 14-bit address bus that could address 16KB of memory. This chip was the centre of what some consider the firsts 'home computer', such as the Mark-8. This chip was basically used in dumb-terminals and general calculators. Later on, the 8008 became the CPU for the very first commercial, not just calculator, personal computers.

Intel 8080 chip

Intel 8080

The Intel 8080 is an early 8-bit microprocessor, released in April 19744 running at 2 MHz, and is generally considered to be "the first truly usable microprocessor" CPU design. It is the successor to the Intel 8008. The architecture of the 8080 strongly influenced the Intel 8086 CPU architecture. The 8080 was used in many early PC, such as the MITS Altair 8800 Computer. The basic architecture of the 8080 and its successors has replaced many proprietary midrange and mainframe computers, and withstood challenges of technologies such as RISC.
