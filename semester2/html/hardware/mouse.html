First Computer Ball Mouse

Telefunken's mouse

The origin

The first computer ball mouse was made by Telefunken in 1968. It was advertised as an optional input device for their computer systems. The German company Telefunken called it "Rollkugel", which means "rolling ball". With this "rolling ball" it was possible to make simple vector graphic operations, like drawing polygons. This was achieved by moving a cursor on a monitor, directed by the "rolling ball".

The Mother Of All Demos

Two months after the technical publications of the company Telefunken, the mouse became famous as an input device. The reason is that it was showcased in the presentation held by Douglas Engelbart, which is still known today as the Mother Of All Demos. There is a recording of this particular demo on Youtube. At 4 minutes and 19 seconds Douglas Engelbart uses the mouse to manipulate the data with a cursor. Later in the video, at 31:02, the mouse is clearly visible.
