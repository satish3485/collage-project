Shooters

Nes Zapper

A shooter is a category of the video game genre. It revolves around the act of shooting something, most commonly a foe, in order to proceed through the game. In these shooting games the player gets his hands on a large array of ranged weapons that can vary from recent weapons such as hand guns, to futuristic weapons like laser rifles.

These types of games tend to be more focused on the players precision and ability to react quickly. These games also touch an important aspect that other genres don't, which is ammunition. This makes it so that the player must be aware of his current ammunition and reinforces the fact that the player needs to be precise with his shots in order to avoid wasting any ammunition.

The origin of shooters

Spacewar
In February 1962, the game Spacewar had been produced at the Massachussets Institute of Technology. Steve Russell was the one behind the code of this game as the designer and programmer of the first version of Spacewar. It was programmed on a computer known as the PDP-1 and is considered the first shooter game. In this two player game you played as one of the two spaceships with the intend of shooting the other down while avoiding colliding with a star and when one of the two spaceships exploded, the other won. Each ship had a limited supply of fuel and missiles and some intricate features like hyperspace which would help the spaceship evade the other one's missiles at the risk of a higher chance of your spaceship exploding. After it's release,this game gained in popularity and began being ported into other systems. Thus, this is not only credited as the first widely available and influential released computer game, but it is also what marked the beginning of the rise of video games.

The evolution of shooters

After the big hit of Spacewar, a lot of new shooting games like Computer Space or Gun Fight were released, the later being a western human vs human shooting game and was the first of its kind. Then, came the game that would mark the beginning of the Golden age of arcade video game, Space Invaders. It was an arcade video game released in 1978 that became extremely popular in Japan and in the U.S.A. In this game, you play as a laser canon with the goal of fending off an alien invasion. the controls are simple, you can move left or right to hide behind covers and you can shoot upwards, however the more aliens you destroy the faster they're going to move, and if they manage to reach Earth you loose.

After it's release a lot of new shooter games began to surge one after the other like Galaxian (1979) or Asteroids and new features were implemented in these shooting games like side-scrolling in Defender (1981) or multiple levels in Scramble (1981). All of these new shooters needed to be grouped, so sub-categories were created;

  • Shoot 'em up like Galaxian, Space Invadersor Xevious.
  • Light gun shooters with the use of the Zapper to shoot ducks in the game Duck Hunt
  • Stealth shooters like Metal Gear
  • First-person shooters like Maze War or Spazism
The list goes on and on, but the point is that these shooting games became more and more popular, and aren't confined in only one category, they have a huge variety of different themes, weapons, gameplay but what they all have in common, is that the player uses mainly ranged weapons.

Maze War

Maze War3
This game was released in 1974 and is considered to be one of the first first-person computer shooting game that inspired a new genre of shooting games. A first-person shooting game is basically game where you play in first-person, this means that you see through the eyes of the character your playing as. This perspective tends to make games more realistic also because they are in 3D and not in 2D like most of the games in that era. In Maze War the player would be put inside of a maze where they could wander around until they find another player (that looked like an eye) and shoot them to gain points. This game could be either played in singleplayer or multiplayer and contained a map so that the player would not get lost. Essentially this can be considered as the first player versus player first-person shooter that inspired games that, thanks to it's concept, inspired new first-person shooters that were getting more and more violent as the graphics got better.

Wolfenstein 3D

Wolfenstein 3D

This first-person shooter game was was developed by id Software and published in 1992 by Apogee Software as a PC game. Inspired by Castle Wolfenstein, this game followed the same plot but had a lot more success receiving many good reviews and sold a lot of copies.

The main plot is quite simple, you play as an American spy with Polish roots called William "B.J." Blazkowicz with the intent to rid the world of the Nazi regime. The story is however divided into three episodes each with their own sub-plot, that each contain levels the player needs to clear in order to proceed. This game is a first-person shooter (or FPS) in which the player fights a variety of enemies including nazis, with a multitude of different weapons but with scarce ammunition. The player can either find ammunition in the levels or pick up guns from fallen enemies, however he needs to watch out for his life bar because if it depletes, he looses not only one of his lifes, but also all of his weapons except for a knife and a pistol. Killing enemies earns the player points that can be transformed into one more life if he reaches 40000 points. The player had then the possibility of choosing his playstyle to be either more of a run and gun or stealthy.

Even though this game was well received by a big part of the gaming community, it got a lot of complaints and was considered as a controversial game by some. The two aspects of this game that were considered controversial were first of the Nazi references with not only having the player fight Nazis in their base but also fighting Hitler as a boss. And since the Second World War was still reminiscent to a lot of people, it was not well recieved. Also the fact that this game was much more violent compared to other games at the time, displaying a lot of blood added fuel to the fire to those who complained about the presence of Nazis.

Mutant rats

This is why this game was censored in certain hardwares like the SNES where all of the Nazi references were removed, blood was replaced by sweat, Hitler wasn't the villain anymore or in the game at all and in the German copies of the game, not only were the angry dogs replaced by mutant rats but the blood wasn't red anymore, it was green. This wasn't the first case of censorship in a video game however this was a very heavy censorship especially for the German version everything is edited so that there not only no signs of Nazis but also no signs of violence towards humans.
