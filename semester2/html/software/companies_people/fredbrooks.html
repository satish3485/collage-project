Frederick Phillips Brooks, Jr.

Frederick Phillips Brooks Jr. is a computer architect, computer scientist and software engineer. He was born on April 19, 1931, in North Carolina. He is famous for his work on IBM System/360, for which he was the chief architect, and for the OS/360 software package. He graduated with a bachelor of Science degree in Physics in 1953, and he did his PhD in Applied Mathematics at Harvard University in 1956.

Brooks won the National Medal of Technology in 1985 and the Turing Award in 1999.

The Mythical Man-Month: Essays on Software Engineering

Adding manpower to a late software project makes it later.
Frederick Brooks

Brooks published the book The Mythical Man-Month in 1975. Its theme is on software engineering and project management, with a point of view based on his work at IBM. It explores the link between the number of people working on a project and the number of hours that they actually work on it. This link is know as Brooks's Law, and it is the core of the book.

The main concept is that the division of work on a big project cannot always be perfect. Therefore, it is important to establish connections between the project's different tasks, which is not always easy to perform in practice.

In his book, Brooks states that assigning more people to a project which is already behind schedule will delay it even more. Basically, the new people hired to work on the project will have to learn about what has been accomplished with the project prior to their arrival, understand the aim of the project, and create their own links inside the work-group.

Brooks showed a mathematical formula to understand how many communication channels are needed in a project, which is n(n-1)/2, where n stands for the number of people working on the project, and the output is the number of communication links. As new people are hired, the communication links increase exponentially because each person needs to create a link with the newcomer, and the newcomer needs to create his/her own link with all the others, consequently complicating the project's communication links.
